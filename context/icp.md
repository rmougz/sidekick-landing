# ICP — Who Sidekick lead magnets are for

The reader every asset should magnetise (and the wrong reader it should repel).

## Primary ICP

**Founders / owners of marketing, creative, and content agencies who are scaling
revenue but can't see — or can't keep — the profit.**

- **Industry:** marketing / creative / content / media agencies (incl. growth,
  social, broadcast, authority/personal-brand agencies). Evidenced clients:
  Kurogo, Literal Humans, Kurve, Be Creative, Everboost, Guided Growth, Tap In
  Media, Authority Agency, Revenue Labz.
- **Size / revenue band:** roughly **$30k–$500k+/month** revenue. Sweet spot is
  the founder who has proven the model and is now scaling (one testimonial:
  "scaled from $300k to $500k MRR while working with Rayhaan").
- **Stage:** past product-market fit, actively scaling, often hiring - and feeling
  the gap between top-line growth and what actually lands in the bank.
- **Geography:** UK-led (Sidekick is UK), but clients span £ and $.

## What keeps them up at night (the problems to mirror)

- "Revenue is growing but I don't know if profit is."
- No visibility on client-level or pod-level profitability; decisions on gut feel.
- Profitable on paper, cash always tight.
- Hiring on panic, not on capacity/data.
- No forward-looking financial layer - everything is backward-looking bookkeeping.
- Wants clarity and lower stress, not another dashboard they won't read.

## The transformation they want

From "growing blind" to **data-led, forward-looking financial control** - knowing
exactly where margin leaks, what each client/pod really costs to serve, and where
to push to grow profit (not just revenue). Endgame for some: a buyer-ready,
exit-optimised business.

## Repel (the wrong reader)

- Pre-revenue or sub-$10k/mo solo freelancers with no team.
- Non-agency businesses (the language, benchmarks, and pod/cost-to-serve framing
  won't fit).
- Founders shopping purely on price for basic compliance bookkeeping - Sidekick
  is the strategic financial layer, not the cheapest tax return.
